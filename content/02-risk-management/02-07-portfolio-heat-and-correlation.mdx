## WHAT IS PORTFOLIO HEAT? (THE TOTAL EXPOSURE)

Let's start with the foundational concept:

PORTFOLIO HEAT = Total capital at risk across ALL open positions

Or more precisely:

<Math>Portfolio Heat = Sum of (Risk per Position √ó Number of Positions)</Math>
<br />

But it's not that simple when correlation enters the picture.

Let me show you the difference between theoretical heat and actual heat.

## THE PORTFOLIO HEAT RULE: 6-10% MAXIMUM

Professional traders follow a strict rule:

NEVER have more than 6-10% of your total capital at risk across ALL positions simultaneously.

This is your maximum portfolio heat limit.

### WHY 6-10%?

**At 6% portfolio heat:**

‚Üí If all positions hit stops simultaneously (worst case)<br/>
‚Üí You lose 6% of your account<br/>
‚Üí Recovery needed: +6.4%<br/>
‚Üí Drawdown: Painful but manageable<br/>
‚Üí Psychological impact: Survivable<br/>

**At 10% portfolio heat:**

‚Üí If all positions hit stops simultaneously<br/>
‚Üí You lose 10% of your account<br/>
‚Üí Recovery needed: +11.1%<br/>
‚Üí Drawdown: Very difficult<br/>
‚Üí Psychological impact: Severe but survivable<br/>

At 20% portfolio heat (violation):

If all positions hit stops simultaneously
You lose 20% of your account
Recovery needed: +25%
Drawdown: Career-threatening
Psychological impact: Potentially terminal

## THE CORRELATION MATRIX: UNVEILING HIDDEN RISK

Now we enter the realm where most retail traders get obliterated:

### CORRELATION

**Correlation = The degree to which two assets move together**

Measured on a scale from -1.0 to +1.0:

‚Üí +1.0 = Perfect positive correlation (move in exact same direction)<br/>
‚Üí 0.0 = No correlation (independent movements)<br/>
‚Üí -1.0 = Perfect negative correlation (move in exact opposite directions)<br/>

## HIDDEN CORRELATION: THE USD TRAP

The most common correlation mistake in forex trading:

Not recognizing that you're trading the SAME CURRENCY in multiple pairs.

### UNDERSTANDING FOREX PAIR STRUCTURE

Every forex pair has TWO currencies:

EUR/USD = Euro / US Dollar

‚Üí Going LONG = You're buying EUR, selling USD<br/>
‚Üí Going SHORT = You're selling EUR, buying USD<br/>

GBP/USD = British Pound / US Dollar

‚Üí Going LONG = You're buying GBP, selling USD<br/>
‚Üí Going SHORT = You're selling GBP, buying USD<br/>

### THE HIDDEN EXPOSURE CALCULATOR

**Question:** If you're long EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and AUD/USD (all 1% risk each), what's your actual USD exposure?

**Answer:**

‚Üí Long EUR/USD = Short 1% USD<br/>
‚Üí Long GBP/USD = Short 1% USD<br/>
‚Üí Long AUD/USD = Short 1% USD<br/>
‚Üí Total USD exposure: Short 3% USD<br/>

You have THREE TIMES the USD exposure you thought.

## IDENTIFYING CORRELATION: THE PRACTICAL METHODS

Let's make this actionable ‚Äî how do you identify correlation BEFORE it destroys you?

### METHOD 1: THE COMMON CURRENCY TEST

**Rule:** If two forex positions share a common currency, they're likely correlated.

**Examples:**

Positions: Long EUR/USD + Long EUR/GBP<br/>

‚Üí Common currency: EUR<br/>
‚Üí If EUR strengthens, BOTH win<br/>
‚Üí If EUR weakens, BOTH lose<br/>
‚Üí Correlation: HIGH (+0.50 to +0.70)<br/>

Positions: Long EUR/USD + Short USD/JPY<br/>

‚Üí Common currency: USD<br/>
‚Üí Both are SHORT USD<br/>
‚Üí If USD strengthens, BOTH lose<br/>
‚Üí If USD weakens, BOTH win<br/>
‚Üí Correlation: HIGH (+0.60 to +0.80)<br/>

Positions: Long EUR/USD + Long GBP/JPY<br/>

‚Üí No common currency<br/>
‚Üí EUR, USD, GBP, JPY all different<br/>
‚Üí Correlation: LOW to MODERATE<br/>

### METHOD 2: THE VISUAL OVERLAY TEST

**Step 1:** Open your charting platform

**Step 2:** Overlay the two instruments you're considering

**Step 3:** Observe:

‚Üí Do they move in the same direction most of the time? ‚Üí High positive correlation<br/>
‚Üí Do they move in opposite directions most of the time? ‚Üí High negative correlation<br/>
‚Üí Do they move independently? ‚Üí Low correlation

**Example:**

EUR/USD (blue line) overlaid with GBP/USD (red line):

‚Üí Both lines rise and fall together<br/>
‚Üí When EUR/USD spikes up, GBP/USD usually spikes up<br/>
‚Üí When EUR/USD drops, GBP/USD usually drops<br/>
‚Üí Conclusion: High correlation (+0.80+)

### METHOD 3: THE CORRELATION COEFFICIENT CALCULATOR

Use a statistical tool (Excel, TradingView, Python) to calculate correlation:

Excel formula:

<Math>=CORREL(Range1, Range2)</Math>

**Example:**

‚Üí Range1: Last 100 daily closes of EUR/USD<br/>
‚Üí Range2: Last 100 daily closes of GBP/USD<br/>
‚Üí Result: +0.87<br/>

**Interpretation:** Very high positive correlation

## BASIC HEDGING TECHNIQUES

Now let's discuss how to actively reduce portfolio heat and correlation risk through hedging.

**HEDGING = Taking offsetting positions to reduce overall exposure**

### HEDGE TYPE 1: DIRECT HEDGE (FOREX)

**Situation:**

‚Üí You're long EUR/USD (1% risk)<br/>
‚Üí News event approaching, high volatility expected<br/>
‚Üí You want to protect the position but not close it<br/>

**Direct hedge:**

‚Üí Open a SHORT EUR/USD position (0.5% risk)<br/>
‚Üí Net exposure: 0.5% long EUR/USD<br/>

**Outcome scenarios:**

**If EUR/USD rises:**

‚Üí Long position: +2% (1:2 RR)<br/>
‚Üí Short hedge: -0.5%<br/>
‚Üí Net: +1.5% ‚úÖ

**If EUR/USD falls:**

‚Üí Long position: -1%<br/>
‚Üí Short hedge: +1% (if it hits 1:2 target)<br/>
‚Üí Net: 0% to +0.5% ‚úÖ

**Purpose:** Reduces risk during uncertain events while maintaining position.

### HEDGE TYPE 2: CORRELATED HEDGE (STOCKS)

**Situation:**

‚Üí You're long AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL (tech stocks, 3% total risk)<br/>
‚Üí Tech sector looking vulnerable<br/>
‚Üí Don't want to close positions (still like individual setups)<br/>

**Correlated hedge:**

‚Üí Short QQQ (NASDAQ ETF) at 1% risk<br/>
‚Üí Net tech exposure: ~2% long (reduced from 3%)

**Outcome:**

**If tech sector rallies:**

‚Üí AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL: +6% combined<br/>
‚Üí QQQ short: -1%<br/>
‚Üí Net: +5%

**If tech sector falls:**

‚Üí AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL: -3% combined<br/>
‚Üí QQQ short: +2%<br/>
‚Üí Net: -1% (protected from -3%)

### HEDGE TYPE 3: INVERSE CORRELATION HEDGE

**Situation:**

‚Üí You're long stocks (risk-on positions)<br/>
‚Üí Market volatility increasing<br/>
‚Üí Want protection without closing positions<br/>

**Inverse hedge:**

‚Üí Long Gold (0.5-1% risk)<br/>
‚Üí Long VIX (volatility index) via options<br/>
‚Üí Long USD (via UUP or DXY futures)<br/>

**Purpose:**

‚Üí Gold typically rises when stocks fall<br/>
‚Üí VIX spikes when stocks crash<br/>
‚Üí USD strengthens during flight-to-safety<br/>

**This is portfolio insurance, not a perfect hedge.**

### HEDGE TYPE 4: OPTIONS HEDGING (ADVANCED)

**Situation:**

‚Üí You're long 100 shares of AAPL at $180 (risk $400 if stop at $176 hit)<br/>
‚Üí Earnings report tonight (high volatility)<br/>

**Options hedge:**

‚Üí Buy 1 PUT option at $175 strike (cost $200)<br/>
‚Üí Maximum loss now: $500 + $200 = $700 (but you have time to exit before $175)<br/>

**Outcome:**

**If AAPL rallies to $200:**

‚Üí Stock gain: $2,000<br/>
‚Üí PUT expires worthless: -$200<br/>
‚Üí Net: +$1,800<br/>

**If AAPL crashes to $160:**

‚Üí Stock loss: -$2,000<br/>
‚Üí PUT gain: +$1,500 (ITM)<br/>
‚Üí Net: -$500 (much better than -$2,000)<br/>

**Note:** Options hedging is complex. Don't attempt without education.
