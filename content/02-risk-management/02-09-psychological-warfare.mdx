## COGNITIVE BIASES: THE HIDDEN SABOTEURS

### BIAS #1: ANCHORING

**In trading:**

â†’ Your brain is anchored to $180 as "fair value"<br/>
â†’ $170 feels "cheap" relative to your entry<br/>
â†’ "It HAS to go back to $180, that's where I bought it!"<br/>

**Reality:**

â†’ The market doesn't care where you bought it<br/>
â†’ $180 is irrelevant; only current price action matters<br/>
â†’ Your anchor is creating irrational holding behavior<br/>

### BIAS #2: CONFIRMATION BIAS

**Definition:** Seeking information that confirms your existing belief while ignoring contradictory evidence.

**In trading:**

â†’ You see bullish chart patterns everywhere<br/>
â†’ You focus on bullish news<br/>
â†’ You dismiss bearish indicators as "noise"<br/>
â†’ You interpret every price move as "about to rally"<br/>

**Reality:**

â†’ Price is making lower lows (bearish)<br/>
â†’ Your bias is blinding you to reality<br/>
â†’ You're ignoring your stop signal<br/>

**Antidote:** Actively seek evidence AGAINST your position. "What would invalidate my thesis?"<br/>

### BIAS #3: RECENCY BIAS

**Definition:** Overweighting recent events and assuming they'll continue.

**In trading:**

**Scenario A:**

â†’ You've won 5 trades in a row<br/>
â†’ Recency bias: "I'm on fire! Everything I touch wins! I should take every setup I see!"<br/>
â†’ Result: Overtrading, overconfidence, eventual crash<br/>

**Scenario B:**

â†’ You've lost 5 trades in a row<br/>
â†’ Recency bias: "My system is broken. Nothing works anymore. I should stop trading."<br/>
â†’ Result: Abandon profitable system during normal variance<br/>

**Antidote:** Track performance over 100+ trades, not last 5. "What's my expectancy over 100 trades?"<br/>

### BIAS #4: HINDSIGHT BIAS

**Definition:** After an event, believing you "knew it all along."

**Reality:**

â†’ At the time, the setup was unclear<br/>

**Antidote:** Journal your thinking BEFORE trades. Refer to contemporaneous notes, not memory.<br/>

**Definition:** Believing that past random events affect future probabilities.

**In trading:**

**Scenario:**

â†’ You've lost 5 trades in a row<br/>
â†’ Gambler's fallacy: "I'm DUE for a win! The next trade HAS to be a winner!"<br/>

**Reality:**
